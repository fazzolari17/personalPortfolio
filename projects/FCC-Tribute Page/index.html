Dr. Jonas Salk

The man who beat polio

A timeline of jonas salk's life

  • October 28, 1914 Jonas Edward Salk was born in New York City, the eldest of three sons to Russian-Jewish immigrants Daniel and Dora Salk.
  • 1939 Earned his medical degree from the New York University School of Medicine
  • 1942 Salk went to the University of Michigan on a research fellowship to develop an influenza vaccine
  • 1947 Salk was appointed director of the Virus Research Laboratory at the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine.
  • With funding from the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis—now known as the March of Dimes—he began to develop the techniques that would lead to a vaccine to wipe out the most frightening scourge of the time: paralytic poliomyelitis
  • 1954 National testing began on one million children, ages six to nine, who became known as the Polio Pioneers.
  • April 12, 1955 The results were announced: the vaccine was safe and effective. The average number of polio cases in the U.S. was more than 45,000. By 1962, that number had dropped to 910
  • 1963 Founding the Salk Institute for Biological Studies in La Jolla it was Salk’s second triumph. He was aided with a $20 million grant from the National Science Foundation and support from the March of Dimes.
  • June 23, 1995 At the age of 80 in La Jolla, California, he passed away.Salk spent his last years searching for a vaccine against AIDS.
